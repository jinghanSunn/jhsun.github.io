Biography

I am a 1st-year Ph.D. student at the Department of Biomedical Engineering, The Chinese University of Hong Kong, under the supervision of Prof. Raymond Tong, supported by HKPFS. Before that, I obtained my M.Phil in Computer Science at Tsinghua University, supervised by Prof. Xiu Li. I received B.Eng in Electronic Engineering and B.Sc in Management with top rank from UESTC. During my master period, I was fortunate to visit the SPL Lab, Brigham and Women's Hospital, Harvard Medical School, and supervised by Prof. Jayender Jagadeesan (2019-2020). I also fortunately worked with Prof.Sandy Wells and Dr.Jie Luo at Harvard. Besides, I also worked at Tencent Jarvis Lab as a research intern (2020-2021) under the supervision of Dr. Yefeng Zheng and Dr. Kai Ma.

My research interest lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence and medical image analysis, specifically in annotation-efficient methods (semi-/weakly-/self-/noisy-label/few-shot learning) and interpretability for uni-/multi-model image registration, segmentation and diagnosis. Collaboration is warmly welcome!

Besides, I enjoy traveling (15+ countries experience, some Vlogs are shared here), SCUBA diving, surfing, badminton, basketball (Fan of LBJ), cocktail, photography and saxophone.
