Biography

I am a year-1 Ph.D. student at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, under the supervision of Prof. Raymond Tong, supported by Hong Kong PhD Fellowship (HKPFS). Before that, I obtained my M.S. in Computer Science at Tsinghua University in 2021, supervised by Prof. Xiu Li. I received B.Eng in Electronic Engineering and B.Sc in Management with top rank from UESTC in 2018. During my master period, I was also a research associate at SPL Lab, Brigham and Women's Hospital, Harvard Medical School (2019-2021), and supervised by Prof. Jayender Jagadeesan. I also fortunately worked closely with Prof. Sandy Wells and Dr. Jie Luo at Harvard. I also joined Tencent Healthcare Jarvis Lab as a research intern (2020-2021), working closely with Dr. Yefeng Zheng (FIEEE) and Dr. Donghuan Lu.

My research interest is driven by real clinical scenario and lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence and medical image analysis, with recent focuses on annotation-efficient learning (semi-/weakly-/self-/few-shot), learning with noisy labels, domain/model generalization and interpretability for various tasks such as registration, segmentation and reconstruction. Developing explicit, simple, yet effective (also efficient) methods is appreciative.

Work Smart, Play Hard: I enjoy traveling (15+ countries experience, some Vlogs are shared here), SCUBA diving (PADI AOW), surfing, badminton, basketball (Fan of LBJ), cocktail, photography and saxophone.
